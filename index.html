"What's my password again?"

With all of the accounts needed for day to day life, it is getting tougher and tougher to maintain and remember strong, unique passwords

We're trying to create a way to manage all these accounts in one place, while also ensuring your own personal security

The Idea

There are many accounts we have to manage these days. Between our work and personal email accounts and social networking, the amount of our personal information on the Internet is continuing to grow. Each account requires a password, and the growing trend among uninformed users is to either use the same password on multiple accounts, create insecure passwords, or even store passwords in plaintext for anyone to see.

In addition, it is getting easier for attackers, through social engineering or technical weaknesses to break through weaker passwords. Two factor authentication, the process of authenticating using a physical object in addition to your password, is a popular way to increase the protection of your accounts.

Our idea is simple: Let our extension manage your account passwords. The extension generates strong passwords for you to use and stores them so that you do not have to remember them. Then, you remember one master password. From there, each time you want to login to an account, you enter your master password and authenticate using your Android device, and the extension will retrieve the appropriate password for you.

It's that easy!

How it Works

There are two parts of the project

Google Chrome Extension

Android App

Once you have installed the extension to Google Chrome and the app to your Android device, and setup the key and master password, here's how easy it is to login to your sites using our system.

Go to the site and click the lock icon. A popup will appear asking for your master password.

After entering your master password, a barcode will appear. Open the Android app, click the "Scan" button, and scan the barcode using your Android device.

A pin will appear. Enter your pin into the extension and the saved password will appear for you to use!
